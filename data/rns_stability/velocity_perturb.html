Velocity perturbation profile for RNS

In paper arXiv:1704.00038, the three velocity is defined

vi=i=(y,x,0)(1)v^i = \Omega \phi^i = \Omega (-y, x,0) \tag{1}vi=i=(y,x,0)(1)

Which is the same velocity used in IllinoisGRMHD.

In HydroBase, the three velocity is defined using Valencia formula,

vvali=uiu0+i=vi+i(2)v_{val}^i = \frac{u^i}{\alpha u^0} + \frac{\beta^i}{\alpha} = \frac{v^i + \beta^i}{\alpha} \tag{2}vvali=u0ui+i=vi+i(2)

From HydroBase velocity to the velocity defined in the paper, the formula is

vi=vvalii=uiu0(3)v^i = \alpha v_{val}^i - \beta^i = \frac{u^i}{u^0} \tag{3}vi=vvalii=u0ui(3)

The velocity perturbation terms we want to add are

wi=[(RxRyA+B)(y),(RyRxA+B)x,0](4)w^i = \left[\left(-\frac{R_x}{R_y}A + B\right) \cdot (-y),\ \left(-\frac{R_y}{R_x}A + B\right)\cdot x,\ 0 \right] \tag{4}wi=[(RyRxA+B)(y),(RxRyA+B)x,0](4)
si=(Dx,Ey,Fz)(5)s^i = (Dx,\ Ey,\ Fz)\tag{5}si=(Dx,Ey,Fz)(5)

With the perturbation, the four velocity UU^\alphaU is

U=u0(1,vi+wi+si)(6) U^\alpha = u^0 (1,\ v^i + w^i + s^i) \tag{6}U=u0(1,vi+wi+si)(6)

To add the perturbation, we will need to change the three velocity viv^ivi to

vperturbi=vi+wi+si(7)v^i_{perturb} = v^i + w^i + s^i \tag{7}vperturbi=vi+wi+si(7)

Additional Diagnostics

The additional diagnostics we want to add are the quadrupole moment and the center of mass

The quadrupole moment is

Iij=(xixj)0u0d3x=(xixj)0wlorentzd3x(8)I^{ij} = \int (x^i \cdot x^j) \rho_0 u^0 \alpha \sqrt{\gamma} d^3 x = \int (x^i \cdot x^j) \rho_0 w_{lorentz} \sqrt{\gamma} d^3 x \tag{8}Iij=(xixj)0u0d3x=(xixj)0wlorentzd3x(8)

and

wlorentz=u0(9)w_{lorentz} = \alpha u^0 \tag{9}wlorentz=u0(9)

The center of mass is

Xcomi=1C0xiu0d3x(10)X_{com}^i = \frac{1}{C_0} \int x^i \rho u^0 \alpha \sqrt{\gamma} d^3 x \tag{10}Xcomi=C01xiu0d3x(10)

Where C0=m0C_0 = m_0C0=m0.

This quantity should be outputed from the Hydro_Analysis when setting hydro_analysis_comp_vol_weighted_center_of_mass.
